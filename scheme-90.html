Karnataka Gram Swaraj Property Tax & e-Khata Initiative

Description: Digital governance reform to improve property tax collection and streamline property records in rural areas. Gram Panchayats can issue property tax assessments, collect payments online, and provide e-Khata (digital property ownership certificates) ensuring transparency and accountability.

Eligibility

  • All rural property owners under Gram Panchayat jurisdiction in Karnataka.
  • Residential, commercial, agricultural, and vacant land properties.
  • Must have valid property ownership documents and Aadhaar-linked records.

Benefits

  • Online system for property tax assessment, payment, and receipts.
  • Issuance of e-Khata for legal recognition of properties.
  • Improved transparency, reduced manual errors, and faster service delivery.
  • Enhanced revenue for Gram Panchayats to fund local development projects.
  • Property owners can access and update records without frequent office visits.

How to Apply

  • Visit the e-Swathu/e-Grama portal: https://gramaswaraj.karnataka.gov.in
  • Register with Aadhaar, mobile number, and property details.
  • Apply online for property tax assessment or e-Khata certificate.
  • Upload documents: property ownership proof, RTC/Pahani, Aadhaar, tax receipts (if applicable).
  • Pay property tax online and download the e-Khata certificate or receipt.

Additional Details

  • Implemented by RDPR, Karnataka.
  • e-Khata serves as legal proof for loans, sales, and registrations.
  • Integration with Seva Sindhu ensures easy access to rural property services.
  • Promotes digital governance and financial self-reliance of Gram Panchayats.
