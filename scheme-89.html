Kudumbashree Micro Enterprise Training (Pilot in Karnataka)

Description: Pilot project in Karnataka to empower rural women and SHG members to start micro-enterprises. Adapted from Kerala‚Äôs Kudumbashree model, it promotes women-led entrepreneurship, financial independence, and livelihood generation.

Eligibility

  • Women members of rural and urban SHGs in Karnataka.
  • Preference to BPL families, SC/ST communities, and minority groups.
  • Age: 18‚Äì55 years.
  • Must be willing to start or expand a small business after training.

Benefits

  • Free training in micro-enterprise management, bookkeeping, and business planning.
  • Support for enterprises in tailoring, food processing, handicrafts, retail, etc.
  • Access to low-interest loans and subsidies through SHGs and cooperative banks.
  • Market linkages through exhibitions, fairs, and government procurement channels.
  • Capacity building for economic self-reliance and leadership roles.

How to Apply

  • Approach local Gram Panchayat office or Kudumbashree facilitation center in pilot areas.
  • Registration via Department of Women and Child Development, Karnataka also possible.
  • Submit Aadhaar, income certificate, SHG membership details, and bank account.
  • After training, apply for micro-enterprise loans and subsidies through cooperative banks or KMDC (for minorities).

Additional Details

  • Implemented as a pilot in selected districts of Karnataka with Kudumbashree Mission, Kerala collaboration.
  • Supported by Department of Rural Development and Panchayat Raj (RDPR), Karnataka.
  • Training modules include skill development, enterprise management, and digital literacy.
  • Focus on promoting women-led rural industries to increase income and reduce migration.
