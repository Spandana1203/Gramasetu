PMGDISHA (Digital Literacy Program)

Description: Launched in 2017 under Digital India, PMGDISHA trains rural citizens in basic digital literacy. The program enables them to use smartphones, computers, internet, e-governance services, and digital payments.

Benefits & Impact

  • Extensive Reach: Over 7.35 crore enrolled, 6.39 crore trained, 4.78 crore certified nationwide
  • In Karnataka: 2,964,726 registered; 2,440,957 trained; 1,833,519 certified
  • Empowers rural citizens to use digital devices, internet, email, e-governance services, and digital payments

Eligibility

  • Rural individuals aged 14‚Äì60 years
  • Focus on women, SC/ST communities, minorities, and differently-abled persons

How to Apply / Train & Get Certified

  • Enroll free at the nearest Common Services Centre (CSC) in your village or Gram Panchayat
  • Complete 20‚Äì30 hours of training on mobile, internet, email, online services, and digital payments
  • Take the certification exam by NIELIT, NIOS, CDAC, etc.
  • Receive digitally signed certificate via DigiLocker

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar Card (mandatory for identification & eKYC)
  • Passport-size Photograph
  • Proof of Residence (if not updated in Aadhaar) ‚Äì Voter ID, Ration Card, or Electricity Bill
  • Mobile number linked with Aadhaar for OTP verification
