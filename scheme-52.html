Prasooti Araike Scheme ‚Äì Maternity Benefit for BPL Women

Description: Karnataka Government scheme providing maternity benefits for pregnant women from Below Poverty Line (BPL) families. Supports access to financial aid, nutrition, and medical services during and after pregnancy to reduce maternal and infant mortality.

Eligibility

  • Resident of Karnataka.
  • Member of a BPL family.
  • Pregnant women registered at Anganwadi or Government Health Center.
  • Preference for deliveries at government or recognized hospitals.
  • Excludes women already receiving maternity benefits under similar schemes.

Benefits

  • Cash Assistance: ‚Çπ3,000 for maternity expenses (subject to change).
  • Healthcare Coverage: Free antenatal checkups, institutional delivery, and postnatal care.
  • Nutrition Support: Encourages nutritious diet for mother and child health.
  • Incentive for Institutional Delivery: Promotes safe hospital deliveries over home deliveries.
  • Reduces Maternal & Infant Mortality Rates (MMR & IMR).

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar card (linked to bank account)
  • BPL ration card
  • Pregnancy registration card (Anganwadi/PHC)
  • Income certificate (if required)
  • Bank account details (Aadhaar-seeded)
  • Medical reports/doctor‚Äôs certificate verifying pregnancy
  • Passport-size photos

How to Apply

  1. Register pregnancy at nearest Anganwadi or Primary Health Center (PHC).
  2. Provide BPL card, Aadhaar, and pregnancy details to the Anganwadi worker.
  3. Information entered into government health database.
  4. After verification, financial benefits credited to Aadhaar-linked bank account under DBT.
  5. Monitor benefits and healthcare services through local Anganwadi or health worker.

Importance

  • Provides economic relief for families unable to afford delivery costs.
  • Improves access to nutrition and healthcare for mothers.
  • Ensures safe and hygienic deliveries, reducing complications.
  • Supports a healthy start for newborns with proper attention and care.
  • Empowers Karnataka's most vulnerable mothers and babies with dignity, health, and safety.
