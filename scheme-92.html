Jal Jeevan Mission ‚Äì Karnataka (Safe Drinking Water Allocation)

Description: Part of India's national program to provide Functional Household Tap Connections (FHTC) to every rural household. Focuses on safe and adequate drinking water, health improvement, and reducing water-fetching burden on women and children.

Eligibility

  • All rural households in Karnataka without functional household tap connections.
  • Priority for SC/ST households, marginalized families, and water-scarce regions.
  • Villages must form Village Water & Sanitation Committees (VWSCs) to manage supply.

Benefits

  • Piped water supply to every rural household.
  • Water quality monitoring and removal of contaminants like fluoride, arsenic, and iron.
  • Women's empowerment through participation in water committees.
  • Improved health by reducing waterborne diseases.
  • Time and effort savings for women and children.

How to Apply

  • No individual application needed; coverage planned at Gram Panchayat level.
  • Households can register via VWSC or Gram Panchayat office.
  • Track applications through official portal.
  • Aadhaar, ration card, and residence proof may be required for connection records.

Additional Details

  • Implemented by Ministry of Jal Shakti, Government of India; executed by RDWSS, Karnataka.
  • Community ownership encouraged for long-term sustainability.
  • Regular water testing via district and block laboratories.
  • Mission target: 100% rural tap water connectivity by 2024.
