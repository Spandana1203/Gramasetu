MGNREGA (National Rural Employment)

Description: MGNREGA, launched in 2005, is a social security scheme by the Government of India. It guarantees at least 100 days of wage employment per year to every rural household whose adult members volunteer to do unskilled manual work. It aims to reduce rural poverty, improve livelihood security, and create rural assets like roads, ponds, and irrigation channels.

Benefits

  • Wage employment for rural households (minimum 100 days/year)
  • Unemployment allowance if work is not provided within 15 days
  • Creation of durable rural assets
  • Women empowerment: at least one-third of beneficiaries must be women
  • Social inclusion: targeted at SC/ST and other vulnerable groups

Eligibility

  • Must be a rural household in India
  • Any adult member willing to do unskilled manual work can apply
  • Priority to marginalized groups (SC/ST, women, landless laborers)

Documents Required

  • Proof of residency (ration card, Aadhaar, voter ID)
  • Photograph of adult household members (for job card)
  • Bank/post office account details for wage payments

How to Apply

  • Visit your local Gram Panchayat office
  • Submit a written application for work (oral applications accepted in some states)
  • Receive a job card with details of household members eligible for work
  • Work allocation is provided within 15 days of application
  • Wages are paid directly to the bank/post office account of the worker
