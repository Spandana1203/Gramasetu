SC/ST Self Employment Scheme

Description: Karnataka Government‚Äôs flagship program to promote entrepreneurship among SC/ST communities. Provides financial assistance, subsidies, and training to start self-employment ventures or small-scale businesses, reducing unemployment and enabling sustainable livelihoods.

Eligibility

  • Must belong to SC or ST community (valid caste certificate required).
  • Permanent resident of Karnataka.
  • Age between 18‚Äì55 years (relaxable as per guidelines).
  • Family income within government-prescribed limit.
  • No default in any financial institution.
  • Must submit a viable business/project plan.

Benefits

  • Subsidized loans through banks and cooperatives.
  • Capital subsidy from Social Welfare Department.
  • Financial assistance for machinery, tools, or raw materials.
  • Skill development and entrepreneurship training.
  • Encourages starting businesses instead of wage employment.
  • Empowers SC/ST youth and women to be self-reliant.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar card (linked with bank account).
  • Caste certificate and income certificate.
  • Residence proof (ration card, voter ID, etc.).
  • Passport-size photographs.
  • Bank passbook copy (Aadhaar-seeded).
  • Simple business plan/project report.
  • Educational qualification certificate (if applicable).

How to Apply

  1. Visit Seva Sindhu Portal ‚Üí https://sevasindhugs.karnataka.gov.in
  2. Register or log in.
  3. Select ‚ÄúApply for Services‚Äù ‚Üí SC/ST Self Employment Scheme.
  4. Fill applicant details, income, caste, and business/project info.
  5. Upload required documents.
  6. Verify Aadhaar OTP and submit.
  7. District committee reviews and recommends eligible cases.
  8. Approved applicants linked with banks for loan sanction and subsidy release.

Importance

  • Promotes economic justice and social equity.
  • Creates new opportunities for marginalized communities.
  • Reduces dependency on wage labor and encourages entrepreneurship.
  • Provides financial freedom and self-reliance.
  • Helps bridge socio-economic gaps and contributes to inclusive rural development.
