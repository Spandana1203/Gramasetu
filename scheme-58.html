Mahila Samruddhi Yojana

Description: Karnataka Government initiative for women-focused economic empowerment, offering loans, subsidies, and guidance for women from economically weaker sections, backward classes, and minorities. Promotes self-reliance through small businesses and income-generating activities.

Eligibility

  • Applicant must be a woman and permanent resident of Karnataka.
  • Belong to economically weaker sections, SC/ST, OBC, or minority communities.
  • Age typically between 18‚Äì55 years.
  • Family income should be within prescribed limits.
  • Must not have defaulted on previous loans.

Benefits

  • Loans with subsidized interest rates.
  • Partial subsidy on sanctioned loan amount.
  • Financial support for setting up micro and small enterprises.
  • Special focus on women from SC/ST, OBC, and minority groups.
  • Training and guidance in entrepreneurship, skill development, and financial management.
  • Improves family income and reduces dependence on unskilled labor.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar card (linked with bank account).
  • Income and caste certificate from competent authority.
  • Residence proof (ration card, voter ID, etc.).
  • Passport-size photographs.
  • Bank passbook copy (Aadhaar-linked).
  • Simple business proposal / plan.
  • SHG membership documents (if applicable).

How to Apply

  1. Visit the official portal of the respective development corporation (e.g., KMDC, KSWDC) or Seva Sindhu Portal ‚Üí https://sevasindhugs.karnataka.gov.in.
  2. Register as a new user or log in.
  3. Select ‚ÄúApply for Services‚Äù ‚Üí Mahila Samruddhi Yojana.
  4. Enter applicant details, income, caste, and business proposal.
  5. Upload required documents in prescribed format.
  6. Complete Aadhaar OTP verification and submit the application.
  7. Note the application number for tracking.
  8. District-level committee reviews; approved applications receive financial sanction.

Importance

  • Encourages women to become job creators rather than job seekers.
  • Strengthens women's role in family and community development.
  • Provides equal opportunity to marginalized women for accessing credit.
  • Enhances self-confidence and independence.
  • Boosts rural and urban economies through support of micro-enterprises.
