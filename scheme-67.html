Krishi Bhagya Scheme (Irrigation for Dry Land Farmers)

Description: Karnataka Government initiative to support dry land farmers with irrigation solutions, water conservation structures, and modern farming practices to improve productivity and income. Encourages farm ponds, polythene lining, micro-irrigation, and cultivation of high-value crops.

Eligibility

  • Farmer and permanent resident of Karnataka.
  • Priority to dry land farmers practicing rainfed agriculture.
  • Own or lease agricultural land suitable for water harvesting.
  • Preference to small/marginal farmers, women, and SC/ST farmers.
  • Willingness to contribute a share of project cost.

Benefits

  • Financial assistance/subsidy for farm ponds and water harvesting structures.
  • Provision of polythene lining to prevent water seepage.
  • Support for drip and sprinkler irrigation systems.
  • Subsidy for solar pumpsets and irrigation equipment.
  • Training and technical support on modern irrigation.
  • Encourages high-value crops like vegetables and fruits.
  • Improves soil moisture conservation and reduces dependency on rainfall.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar card (linked with bank account).
  • RTC / land ownership documents.
  • Residence proof (ration card, voter ID, etc.).
  • Bank account details (Aadhaar-seeded passbook).
  • Caste & income certificate (if applicable).
  • Passport-size photographs.
  • Application form with farmer‚Äôs self-declaration.

How to Apply

  1. Visit nearest Raitha Samparka Kendra (RSK) or Agriculture Department office.
  2. Collect and fill out the Krishi Bhagya Scheme application form.
  3. Attach required documents including land records and bank details.
  4. Submit to concerned officer for verification.
  5. Upon approval, receive technical guidance and subsidy for farm ponds/irrigation systems.
  6. Subsidy transferred to Aadhaar-linked bank account.
  7. Apply online via Seva Sindhu Portal if desired.
  8. Track status through local Agriculture Department offices or portal.

Importance

  • Provides sustainable irrigation in drought-prone areas.
  • Reduces crop losses due to rainfall failure.
  • Encourages water conservation and efficient utilization.
  • Enhances income through crop diversification.
  • Contributes to food security and rural prosperity.
