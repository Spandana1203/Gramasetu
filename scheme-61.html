Vatsalya Scheme ‚Äì Health Aid for Children with Heart Defects

Description: Karnataka Government healthcare initiative providing free or subsidized medical treatment for children suffering from congenital heart defects or severe cardiac conditions. Financial aid ensures no child is denied treatment due to lack of funds.

Eligibility

  • Child must be a permanent resident of Karnataka.
  • Typically covers children below 18 years.
  • Family must belong to BPL category (priority).
  • Children must have doctor-certified congenital heart defects or life-threatening cardiac conditions.
  • Income and other eligibility conditions as per Health & Family Welfare Department.

Benefits

  • Free or subsidized heart surgeries and treatment for children below 18 years.
  • Covers hospitalization, surgery, medicines, and follow-up care.
  • Empanelled government and private hospitals across Karnataka.
  • Direct payment to hospitals reducing out-of-pocket expenses.
  • Special focus on children from BPL and marginalized families.
  • Improves survival chances and quality of life.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar cards of child and parents.
  • BPL ration card or income certificate.
  • Medical diagnosis/report from recognized hospital/doctor.
  • Referral slip from government health authorities (if required).
  • Child‚Äôs birth certificate/age proof.
  • Bank details of parent/guardian (for reimbursement if applicable).
  • Passport-size photographs of child and parents.

How to Apply

  1. Visit nearest Government Hospital/Primary Health Centre (PHC) for screening and referral.
  2. Obtain medical certification that surgery/treatment is required.
  3. Submit application and documents to District Health Officer (DHO) or Taluk Health Officer (THO).
  4. Eligible children referred to empanelled hospitals.
  5. Treatment/surgery carried out free or with subsidy at designated hospitals.
  6. Parents can track application/treatment approval via Health Department or Seva Sindhu portal.

Importance

  • Saves children‚Äôs lives through timely medical intervention.
  • Supports poor families facing catastrophic health expenses.
  • Reduces infant and child mortality from congenital heart diseases.
  • Promotes healthcare equity for vulnerable families.
  • Builds a healthier future generation for Karnataka.
