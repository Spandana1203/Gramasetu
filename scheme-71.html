Ganga Kalyana Scheme (Irrigation Borewell Subsidy)

Description: Karnataka Government program providing borewell and lift irrigation facilities to small and marginal farmers, primarily SC/ST and other backward communities. Supports drilling, pump installation, and lift irrigation to ensure a permanent water source and improve farm productivity.

Eligibility

  • Permanent resident of Karnataka.
  • Belong to SC/ST or other backward communities (caste certificate required).
  • Small and marginal farmers with agricultural land prioritized.
  • Must own suitable land for borewell or lift irrigation.
  • Family income within prescribed scheme limits.
  • Should not have availed similar irrigation benefits previously.

Benefits

  • Subsidy for borewell drilling and pump set installation.
  • Financial support for lift irrigation where groundwater is not feasible.
  • Assistance for construction of water storage tanks in select cases.
  • Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) to Aadhaar-linked bank accounts.
  • Reduces dependency on monsoon rains and mitigates drought-related risks.
  • Priority for SC/ST small and marginal farmers.
  • Promotes better crop yields and diversified farming.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar card (linked with bank account).
  • RTC / land ownership documents.
  • Caste certificate (SC/ST/OBC).
  • Income certificate issued by competent authority.
  • Residence proof (ration card, voter ID, etc.).
  • Bank passbook copy (Aadhaar-seeded).
  • Passport-size photographs.
  • Application form with farmer‚Äôs self-declaration.

How to Apply

  1. Visit nearest Raitha Samparka Kendra (RSK) or District Social Welfare Office.
  2. Collect and fill Ganga Kalyana Scheme application form.
  3. Attach land records, Aadhaar, caste certificate, and bank details.
  4. Submit to Agriculture/Social Welfare officer for verification.
  5. Officials inspect land and approve borewell or lift irrigation projects.
  6. Subsidy released via DBT to Aadhaar-linked bank account.
  7. Apply and track online via Seva Sindhu Portal.

Importance

  • Provides assured irrigation for small and marginal farmers.
  • Reduces crop losses due to drought or uncertain rainfall.
  • Enhances productivity and encourages crop diversification.
  • Improves farmer income and livelihood security.
  • Ensures equal access to irrigation for SC/ST farmers.
