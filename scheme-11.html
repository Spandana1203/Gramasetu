National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP)

Description: NRDWP is a central government scheme launched in 2009 to provide safe and adequate drinking water to rural communities across India. It focuses on sustainable, equitable, and safe water supply and promotes community participation in water management.

Benefits

  • Safe and adequate drinking water to rural households
  • Piped water supply, handpumps, and wells depending on local needs
  • Water quality monitoring and treatment facilities
  • Community participation in water management
  • Capacity building for Panchayats and local institutions

Eligibility

  • Rural households in India, especially those in water-scarce areas
  • Priority to BPL families, SC/ST communities, and remote villages lacking potable water

Documents Required

  • Proof of residence (ration card, Aadhaar, voter ID)
  • Community representation letter if applying on behalf of a village committee
  • Any prior water quality report (if available)

How to Apply / Access Benefits

  • Visit your local Gram Panchayat or Panchayat Raj Department office. panchayat.gov.in
  • Submit a request for drinking water supply or infrastructure support
  • Community-based committees (Village Water and Sanitation Committees) assess requirements
  • Projects are implemented under NRDWP guidelines with government funding
  • Maintenance and monitoring is done locally by the Panchayat or Village Water and Sanitation Committee
