Pashu Bhagya Scheme (Livestock Development)

Description: Karnataka Government flagship program supporting livestock farmers through financial assistance, subsidies, and infrastructure support. Encourages dairy, sheep/goat, piggery, poultry, and fisheries for income diversification and improved rural livelihoods.

Eligibility

  • Permanent resident of Karnataka.
  • Farmer engaged in or willing to start livestock-related activity.
  • Priority to BPL families, women, and SC/ST communities.
  • Must have basic facilities/land to set up animal sheds or ponds.
  • Must not be a defaulter in any government loan or subsidy program.

Benefits

  • Financial assistance and subsidy for dairy, sheep/goat, piggery, poultry, and fisheries.
  • Subsidy for construction of cattle sheds, poultry sheds, and fish ponds.
  • Support for purchase of high-yielding breeds and small ruminants.
  • Assistance for fodder development and water supply to animals.
  • Veterinary care and vaccination support.
  • Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) to Aadhaar-linked bank accounts.
  • Encourages women, SC/ST, and marginal farmers to take up animal husbandry.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar card (linked with bank account).
  • BPL card or income certificate (for subsidy eligibility).
  • RTC / land ownership proof.
  • Residence proof (ration card, voter ID, etc.).
  • Bank passbook copy (Aadhaar-seeded).
  • Caste certificate (for SC/ST beneficiaries).
  • Passport-size photographs.
  • Simple project/business plan for livestock activity.

How to Apply

  1. Visit the nearest Department of Animal Husbandry & Veterinary Services (AHVS) office or Raitha Samparka Kendra (RSK).
  2. Collect and fill out Pashu Bhagya Scheme application form.
  3. Attach necessary documents including Aadhaar, land records, and bank details.
  4. Submit the application to the concerned officer for verification.
  5. Officials inspect the site and recommend subsidy sanction.
  6. Subsidy/assistance released to Aadhaar-linked bank account via DBT.
  7. Apply and track online via Seva Sindhu Portal.

Importance

  • Encourages income diversification beyond crop farming.
  • Improves milk, meat, and egg production in Karnataka.
  • Empowers women and marginalized groups through livestock-based livelihoods.
  • Reduces rural poverty by creating additional income sources.
  • Strengthens food and nutritional security in villages.
