Bhagyalakshmi Scheme (Girl Child Welfare)

Description: Karnataka Government initiative to promote birth and education of girl children in BPL families. Initial financial deposit is made in the girl's name at birth, growing into a maturity benefit at age 18. Encourages education, discourages child marriage, and ensures long-term financial security.

Eligibility

  • Girl child born in a BPL family in Karnataka.
  • Maximum two girl children per family.
  • Child‚Äôs birth must be officially registered.
  • Child must be immunized and enrolled in school.
  • Girl must remain unmarried until 18 years to claim maturity benefit.
  • Parents and child must be permanent residents of Karnataka.

Benefits

  • Initial financial deposit in the girl child‚Äôs name (insurance/deposit scheme).
  • Maturity benefit of approx. ‚Çπ1,00,000 at age 18 (if unmarried).
  • Annual scholarships from 1st to 10th standard.
  • Insurance cover in case of accidental death/disability of guardian.
  • Promotes education, prevents child marriage, provides financial independence.
  • DBT of scholarship benefits to Aadhaar-linked bank account.

Documents Required

  • Parents' and girl child‚Äôs Aadhaar cards.
  • BPL ration card.
  • Girl child‚Äôs birth certificate.
  • Immunization card.
  • School admission/enrollment certificate.
  • Bank account details (Aadhaar-linked).
  • Passport-size photographs.

How to Apply

  1. Visit nearest Anganwadi Centre or Women & Child Development (WCD) office.
  2. Collect and fill Bhagyalakshmi Scheme application form.
  3. Attach BPL card, birth certificate, Aadhaar, and other required documents.
  4. Submit via Anganwadi worker or CDPO for verification and approval.
  5. Financial assistance deposited in girl child‚Äôs name; scholarships/maturity benefits released as per eligibility.
  6. For online details and updates, visit Seva Sindhu Portal.

Importance

  • Encourages families to value and nurture girl children.
  • Promotes education and prevents child marriage.
  • Provides long-term financial security to daughters.
  • Reduces economic burden of raising girl child for poor families.
  • Fights gender bias and empowers women from an early stage.
