Karnataka Handloom Development Programme

Description: The scheme promotes Karnataka's traditional handloom industry while supporting weavers and artisans with financial assistance, modern tools, training, and market access. It helps adopt modern technology, ensures sustainable raw materials, and creates market linkages nationally and internationally.

Eligibility

  • Registered handloom weaver or artisan in Karnataka.
  • Permanent resident of Karnataka.
  • Proof of occupation as weaver/artisan.
  • Priority to SC/ST, women, and economically weaker handloom workers.
  • Age 18‚Äì60 years (as per guidelines).

Benefits

  • Financial assistance/subsidy for looms, raw materials, and equipment.
  • Support for modernization and adoption of new technology.
  • Training in modern design, dyeing, weaving, and marketing.
  • Cluster development initiatives for strong handloom communities.
  • Market access through exhibitions, fairs, and online platforms.
  • Assistance for branding and certification of handloom products.
  • Special incentives for women weavers.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar card (linked with bank account).
  • Weaver ID or proof of handloom activity (Handloom Dept./association).
  • Residence proof (ration card, voter ID, electricity bill, etc.).
  • Caste & income certificate (if applicable).
  • Bank passbook copy (Aadhaar-seeded).
  • Passport-size photographs.
  • Business proposal/requirement list (for subsidy/loan schemes).

How to Apply

  1. Visit Department of Handlooms & Textiles website.
  2. Register as a weaver or log in.
  3. Select desired handloom development scheme (loom subsidy, raw material, training, etc.).
  4. Fill applicant and occupational details.
  5. Upload required documents.
  6. Complete Aadhaar OTP verification and submit.
  7. District Handloom Officer verifies; approved beneficiaries receive DBT or in-kind assistance.

Importance

  • Preserves traditional weavers‚Äô livelihoods.
  • Helps artisans compete in modern markets.
  • Promotes ‚ÄúMake in Karnataka‚Äù and strengthens rural economies.
  • Empowers women and marginalized communities in weaving sector.
  • Preserves Karnataka‚Äôs centuries-old textile and cultural traditions.
