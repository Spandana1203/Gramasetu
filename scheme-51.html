Namma Clinics ‚Äì Urban & Rural Health Services

Description: Government of Karnataka initiative to provide low-cost, accessible primary healthcare to urban and rural communities. Clinics act as one-stop centers for consultation, diagnosis, and treatment while reducing pressure on larger hospitals.

Eligibility

  • All Karnataka residents, with priority to BPL families, urban poor, and rural populations.
  • Priority to women, children, elderly, and those with chronic illnesses.
  • No income certificate required for primary care.
  • Valid Aadhaar or residence proof for registration.

Benefits

  • Free primary healthcare including consultation, diagnosis, and minor ailment treatment.
  • Maternal & child health services: immunization, antenatal & postnatal care, nutrition counseling.
  • Health screenings for blood pressure, diabetes, cancer, and infectious diseases.
  • Free essential medicines and diagnostic tests.
  • Health education on hygiene, nutrition, family planning, and disease prevention.
  • Accessible clinics in urban slums and rural areas to ensure universal coverage.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar card (for patient registration)
  • Residence proof (ration card, voter ID, etc.)
  • Health records (if prior treatment history exists)
  • BPL card (for extended benefits under linked schemes)

How to Apply / Access Services

  1. Locate the nearest Namma Clinic in your urban ward or rural panchayat.
  2. Register at the help desk with Aadhaar/residence details.
  3. Consult the on-duty doctor for diagnosis and treatment.
  4. Receive free medicines and diagnostic tests as prescribed.
  5. For specialized care, referrals are made to higher government hospitals.
  6. Participate in periodic health camps and awareness programs conducted by the clinic.

Importance

  • Minimizes out-of-pocket medical expenses for families.
  • Reduces overcrowding in government hospitals.
  • Enhances early detection and treatment of chronic diseases.
  • Encourages healthier communities with better primary healthcare infrastructure.
  • Especially transformative in urban slums and rural villages lacking easy access to doctors and medicines.
