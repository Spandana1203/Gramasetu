Karnataka Sakala Services Act

Description: Enacted in 2011 and operational from 2012, the Sakala Services Act guarantees time-bound delivery of public services to citizens in Karnataka.

Benefits

  • Ensures timely delivery of government services
  • Covers over 478 services across 47 departments (caste/income certificates, birth/death registrations, driving licenses, ration cards, etc.)
  • Citizens can track applications and file appeals if services are delayed or rejected
  • Penalties for officials: ‚Çπ20 per day for delayed services (up to ‚Çπ500), partly payable to the citizen

Eligibility

  • All citizens of Karnataka using services listed under the Sakala Act
  • No separate registration needed; eligibility is automatic when applying for a Sakala-covered service

How to Apply

  • Visit the relevant Sakala-enabled government office (Sakala Portal)
  • Submit the required application and documents
  • Receive a 15-digit GSC number to track your application
  • Track the status through the official portal or SMS service
  • If delayed or rejected, file an appeal under the grievance redress mechanism
