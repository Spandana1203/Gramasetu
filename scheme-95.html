Solar Micro-Grids for Gram Panchayats under PPP ‚Äì Karnataka

Description: Initiative under PPP model to provide reliable, clean electricity in rural areas. Micro-grids ensure continuous power supply for households, schools, health centers, and community services while reducing dependence on conventional energy sources.

Eligibility

  • All Gram Panchayats in Karnataka are eligible.
  • Priority to power-deficient, remote, and backward villages.
  • Implemented under PPP with private solar companies, Panchayats, and state government.

Benefits

  • 24x7 electricity for rural households and public institutions.
  • Promotes renewable energy adoption and reduces carbon footprint.
  • Supports digital initiatives like e-Khata, e-learning, and digital libraries.
  • Local employment opportunities in solar installation and maintenance.
  • Reduces long-term government expenditure on rural power subsidies.

How to Apply

  • Gram Panchayats propose solar micro-grid projects through RDPR.
  • Submit applications to KREDL.
  • Required documents: Panchayat resolution, land availability, and community approval.
  • After approval, private partners set up solar plants and grids with partial funding/subsidy from government.
  • Villagers access electricity via prepaid/postpaid community billing systems.

Additional Details

  • Implemented by MNRE, in collaboration with KREDL and private solar firms.
  • Funded under PPP with government subsidies and private sector investment.
  • Solar micro-grids typically range from 10kW to 250kW depending on village size.
  • Promotes sustainable rural development powering schools, water supply systems, and digital services.
