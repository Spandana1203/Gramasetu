Stree Shakti Groups ‚Äì Empowerment of Women

Description: Karnataka Government flagship initiative empowering women via Self-Help Groups (SHGs). Focuses on financial self-reliance, skill upgradation, and social empowerment.

Eligibility

  • All women residents of Karnataka.
  • Must join or establish a Self-Help Group (SHG).
  • Priority to BPL women, widows, single mothers, and backward-class women.
  • SHG members to make small regular savings toward group fund.

Benefits

  • Access to micro-credit: low-interest loans for self-employment and small enterprises.
  • Income generation: activities like tailoring, dairy farming, handicrafts, food processing.
  • Skill development: training on entrepreneurship, financial literacy, digital skills, vocational trades.
  • Empowerment in decision-making: confidence in family and community decisions.
  • Government linkages: priority access to welfare schemes, subsidies, and grants.
  • Social development: awareness on health, education, child rights, women‚Äôs rights.
  • Collective strength: solidarity, networking, and leadership among women.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar card for each member
  • Residence proof (voter ID, electricity bill, ration card, etc.)
  • BPL card (if applicable)
  • Passport-sized photographs
  • Bank account information (Aadhaar-linked)
  • SHG registration documents (for group certification)

How to Apply

  1. Join or form a SHG in your locality (ward/village).
  2. Register the SHG with Department of Women & Child Development or NGO facilitators.
  3. Open joint bank account in the name of the SHG for savings and credit transactions.
  4. Enroll under the scheme via local Stree Shakti Coordinator or Women & Child Development Office.
  5. Apply for loans, subsidies, and training programs via SHG network.
  6. Attend monthly meetings, workshops, and training sessions conducted under the scheme.
  7. Official portal: Department of Women & Child Development

Importance

  • Transforms homemakers into entrepreneurs.
  • Makes women financially independent.
  • Builds confidence and leadership in rural and urban women.
  • Promotes grassroots social reform via women empowerment initiatives.
  • Beyond savings and loans, creates a movement enabling women as agents of change.
